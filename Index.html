HISTOIRE DU TIGRE

The monologue begins with the story of the Long March and
of a soldier of the Communist rearguard who is wounded in the leg. The
wound becoming infected and gangrenous, the smell is unbearable to his
comrades and the wound slows them down, and the soldier obtains not to
be finished but to be left on the way.

He then gains a cave, forced by torrential rains. The cave is revealed to be
inhabited by a tigress and two tigons, one of which has just died in the
weather. But the soldier stinks too much for them to eat him. The tigress
has breasts full of milk and her tiger, full of water, refuses to suckle her;
she urges the man to drink her milk, and the man does so, drinking from
one, then two, then four breasts. Sated, he falls asleep. The next day the
scene is repeated and this time the tigress licks her wound and spits out
the gangrene. In a few days, the wound is purified and the blood flows
normally again. The tigress then brings back the fruit of its hunt. Man gets
a scrap of it, which he cooks and which then pleases both animals. The
soldier then becomes a cook in the service of the animal family, but, tired,
runs away on a rainy day.
